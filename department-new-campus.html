New Campus

Building the future home of ICF Cambodia — coordinating the planning, construction, and fundraising for the new campus that will serve the next generation of our mission.

Lead: Annina Huembeli Team: 2 staff ED: ND Strupler

Let's build the future

2025 was a year of significant progress. In partnership with PETRAM Design International, the master plan and updated 3D visualisations give a clear picture of the new campus — stepping forward from concept design into detailed design in 2026. By the end of 2025, all administrative documentation was finalised and the building permit application was officially submitted.

See full master plan

What We Do

Campus Development
Overseeing the planning, design, and construction of ICF Cambodia's new ~12,000 m² campus — a landmark investment in the long-term future of the organisation. In partnership with PETRAM Design International.
Fundraising for New Campus
Coordinating dedicated fundraising efforts — total budget USD 6.18M (Phase 1: USD 4.98M · Phase 2: approx. USD 1.2M). Working with donors, foundations, and partners worldwide.
Project Management & Reporting
Tracking construction milestones, budgets, timelines, and contractor relationships — keeping all stakeholders informed throughout the build.

Why the extended planning phase? Before construction begins, all essential elements are clearly defined so contracts can be negotiated solid, uncertainties minimised, and unexpected costs reduced — resulting in a faster and more efficient build.

Construction Phases

Phase 1 — Launch & Foundation
USD 4.98M
• Two event halls (400 + 350 seats) for events, seminars, and sports
• Education Center — classrooms for training, tutoring, workshops
• Daycare for 50 children (expandable to 100 in Phase 2)
• Drink Water Station providing clean water to the community
Phase 2 — Expansion & Completion
~USD 1.2M
• Daycare expansion to 100 children
• Office building with meeting rooms
• Bistro & entrance area — a place for community and encounters
• Play and sports facilities · Parks and landscape design
• Crematorium as a social service for the local community
